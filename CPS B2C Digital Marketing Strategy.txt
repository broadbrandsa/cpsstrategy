1. Executive Summary
This strategy positions Cornerstone Performance Solutions to achieve its B2C enrollment targets of 35 students per qualification per intake across two programmes (HCIB and ACL6), with a target cost per acquisition of R400 per enrolled student. Based on our comprehensive audit, five workshop sessions with the CPS leadership team, and analysis of the competitive landscape, we have identified that CPS’s core challenge is not product quality — it’s the gap between the strength of the education offering and the weakness of the student-facing experience that surrounds it.
The current digital presence speaks to organisations, not individuals. Prospective students cannot find clear answers to their most fundamental questions: What will I learn? What will it cost? What career will it lead to? How long will it take? This strategy addresses these gaps through a coordinated approach across six workstreams: audience psychology, website CRO, messaging and copy, content strategy, paid media, and a phased launch plan tied to the May 10 intake.
With a starting media budget of R15,000/month across Google and Meta, scaling with traction, and two intakes per year to start, this plan is designed to be lean, measurable, and built for compounding returns.
2. Situation Analysis
2.1 Where CPS Stands Today
CPS is a 25-year-old education company in strategic transition. Under new leadership, it is shifting from a predominantly B2B model (serving banks and financial institutions) to building a sustainable B2C direct-to-student channel. The business currently derives approximately 70% of revenue from B2B and targets 30% from B2C.
2.2 The B2C Challenge
No historical B2C performance data exists — the previous agency retained all analytics and IP
The current website was a quick rebuild (Next.js on Vercel) but is not optimised for B2C conversion
Google Ads are running but generic — focused on NQF levels and SAQA IDs rather than career outcomes
No Meta (Facebook/Instagram) ads are currently active
Lead nurturing flows do not exist — some form submissions receive no confirmation email
The application form is an 8-step process with a R300 fee, creating significant friction
Brand awareness among individual students is low — CPS is known in banking boardrooms, not on social media
2.3 What CPS Has Going For It
Proprietary learning platform (CPSLearn) with AI-powered personalised learning, reflection tools, and AI marking — no competitor has this integration level
BEE Level 1 accreditation — critical for corporate clients and students seeking SETA funding
11,400+ professionals trained across SA’s biggest banks (Absa, Standard Bank, FNB, Capitec, Nedbank, etc.)
Brandon Hall Awards for learning excellence — equivalent to the Oscars in L&D
Competitive pricing: approximately 20% below major competitors
Council on Higher Education and QCTO accreditation on qualifications
Trademarked methodologies: RAMPed, Business Impact Learning, Digital Process Facilitation
Fastest assessment turnaround in market (AI marking: 2–3 minutes vs 45–60 minutes human)
3. Target Audience & Decision Psychology
3.1 Primary Personas
Persona 1: The Banking Starter (HCIB)
Demographics: Age 22–32, matric graduate or career changer, based in Gauteng/WC/KZN metros, currently employed in entry-level banking or retail, or unemployed seeking entry into financial services.
Psychographics: Ambitious but uncertain. Fears wasting money on a qualification that won’t lead anywhere. Researches extensively before committing. Compares CPS against Milpark, MANCOSA, Regent, and university options. Price-sensitive but willing to invest if the outcome is clear. Motivated by career mobility and financial independence.
Jobs to Be Done: "Help me get a real qualification that banks actually recognise, so I can build a career in banking and earn more."
Persona 2: The Aspiring Leader (ACL6)
Demographics: Age 28–40, currently employed in financial services or professional services, 3–7 years work experience, seeking management/leadership roles, based in SA metros.
Psychographics: Has hit a career ceiling. Knows they need formal credentials to advance. Values practical, applicable learning over academic theory. Time-poor and needs flexibility. Wants to be taken seriously as a leader and not just be "another employee."
Jobs to Be Done: "Help me prove I’m ready for management and get the qualification that unlocks the next level."

4. Website & Conversion Strategy
4.1 Current State Assessment
The audit identified that CPS’s website is built for organisations, not students. The homepage speaks to "adaptive education solutions" and "capability development" — language that resonates in a boardroom but creates confusion for a 25-year-old looking to start a banking career. Key findings:
No student-facing value proposition on the homepage
No segmentation for B2B vs B2C audiences
Critical information (pricing, modules, outcomes) hidden behind forms or in PDF brochures
Mobile performance score of 44/100 (due to heavy Divi theme, duplicate scripts, unoptimised images)
Inconsistent form types across qualifications (multi-step, Pipedrive, pop-ups, generic contact forms)
No email confirmation on some form submissions — students feel "scammed"
Multiple competing CTAs with no clear hierarchy

4.3 Landing Page Strategy
Each qualification needs a dedicated landing page optimised for paid traffic. These pages must operate independently from the main site navigation and follow a single-path conversion architecture:
Landing Page Blueprint (per qualification)
Hero section: Outcome-focused headline + qualification badge + single CTA
Social proof bar: Graduate count, banking partner logos, accreditation badges
"Is this for you?" section: Target persona description, prerequisites, time commitment
Programme structure: Module overview with expandable details (not hidden in PDFs)
Career outcomes: Specific roles this leads to, salary benchmarks where available
Pricing section: Monthly payment prominently displayed, total cost, payment options, comparison to competitors
Student stories: 2–3 testimonials with photos, names, and outcomes
FAQ section: Address top 5 objections (cost, time, difficulty, recognition, support)
Sticky CTA: "Download Info Pack" (soft) and "Apply Now" (hard) — dual conversion paths
4.4 Conversion Funnel Design
The current funnel pushes directly from ad to application. This creates friction because students are not yet committed. The redesigned funnel introduces a micro-conversion step:
Awareness → Paid ad / organic content → Interest → Landing page with dual CTA → Consideration → Info pack download / career quiz (captures email + phone) → Nurture → Email + WhatsApp sequence (3–5 touchpoints over 7–14 days) → Application → Simplified form → Enrollment → Admissions follow-up + payment setup

7. Paid Media Strategy
7.1 Budget & Allocation
Starting budget: R15,000/month, increasing with traction. We recommend a 50/50 split between Google and Meta to start, shifting based on early performance data. This balanced approach lets us capture high-intent search demand while simultaneously building awareness and pipeline through social.

7.2 Google Ads Strategy
Campaign Structure
We recommend three campaign types, launched in phases:
Campaign 1: Brand Search (Always on)
Keywords: "CPS", "Cornerstone Performance Solutions", "CPS banking", "CPS HCIB"
Purpose: Capture branded searches and protect against competitors bidding on CPS name
Budget: R1,000/month (low CPC expected)
Campaign 2: High-Intent Non-Brand (Core)
Keywords: "higher certificate in banking", "banking qualification online", "NQF 5 banking course", "leadership qualification South Africa", "advanced certificate in leadership", "banking course online SA"
Match types: Phrase and exact match only — avoid broad match initially
Ad extensions: Sitelinks (Pricing, Modules, Apply Now, Download Info Pack), Callouts (Accredited, 100% Online, From R2,480/month), Structured snippets
Budget: R5,500/month
Campaign 3: Competitor Conquesting (Phase 2)
Keywords: Competitor names + qualification terms (Milpark banking, MANCOSA leadership, Regent banking course)
Purpose: Intercept students comparing options
Ad copy: Focus on CPS differentiators (price, flexibility, bank partnerships)
Budget: R1,000/month

7.4 CPA Modelling
Working backwards from the R400 CPA target with a 50/50 Google/Meta split. Note: Google CPCs in the SA education vertical typically range R12–R20, while Meta CPCs are significantly lower at R5–R8. The blended CPC benefits from Meta’s lower cost per click driving volume.

Key Takeaways from the Model
The Target scenario (R816 CPA) exceeds the 70-enrollment-per-intake goal with 110 enrollments over 6 months. This provides margin even if lead quality or conversion rates underperform initially.
The Conservative scenario (R2,857 CPA) falls short at 32 enrollments per intake. This scenario reflects poor landing page conversion (4%) and low lead-to-enrollment rates (10%) — conditions we can avoid through proper CRO, nurture sequences, and admissions follow-up.
At the R400 CPA target, R15k/month would produce approximately 38 enrollments per month, or 225 per intake — 3x the target. This means the R400 CPA is achievable if the funnel performs at or above Target assumptions.
The critical levers are: (1) landing page conversion rate (CRO work in Section 4), (2) lead-to-enrollment rate (nurture sequences in Section 8), and (3) CPC management through ad quality and relevance.
At R400 CPA, acquiring 70 students costs R28,000 in total media. With R90,000 available per intake (R15k × 6 months), there is R62,000 of headroom for awareness building, retargeting, and content amplification beyond direct acquisition.
